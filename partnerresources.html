Messaging We Discourage

Fear-driven marketing

Reason Why:
Our industry is inundated by the negative effects of fear-driven marketing. From competitors hit with deceptive advertising lawsuits to the growth of data breach fatigue among consumers and small business owners. Moreover, our goal is to empower customers to take control — not frighten them.

Example of fear-driven marketing

“If you don’t have identity protection, you should feel scared.”

Alternative Approach:
Empowering people to take steps to protect themselves from ID Crimes.

Alternative Example that EZShield approves

“Secure Your Identity”

The phrase “Peace of Mind”

Reason Why:
The phrase “peace of mind” is commonplace in many industries, to the point that its meaning has become diluted. So we have been moving away from using it. We want to differentiate ourselves from our competitors, and this only serves to make us blend in.

Example of "Peace of Mind"

“Experience Peace of Mind with EZShield Identity Protection”

Alternative Approach:
Encourage a proactive approach to data protection. As part of this stance, we developed the tagline “Secure Your Identity.”

Messaging We Do Not Support

Making people feel powerless

Reason Why:
Positive, empowering messages help people feel connected to their services and lead to long-term engagement. While powerless consumers are often the first to purchase identity protection services, they are also the first to leave once the fear of identity theft is no longer top-of-mind.

Example of making people feel powerless

“Without identity protection, there is no way to protect your identity.”

Alternative Approach:
Motivate people to make a difference through better security practices, monitoring tools and resolution specialist.

Using Statistics

Intro to Statistics

WHY GAP

Statistics You Can Use

  • There were 38.3 million identity crime victims in 2015 (that’s one in eight Americans)
    2016 Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • There is a new identity theft victim every 30 seconds
    2015 Data Breach Report, Identity Theft Resource Center
  • There is a new payment fraud victim every 2 seconds
    2016 Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • Every second, five sensitive files are breached
    2015 Data Breach Report, Identity Theft Resource Center
  • 1 in 5 data breach victims ultimately experienced fraud
    2016 Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • Consumers lost $15 billion to fraud in 2015 (not out-of-pocket loss)
    2016 Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • The average identity theft victim was defrauded of $2,379 (not out-of-pocket loss)
    2016 Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • In 2015, more than 250,000 small businesses were targets of fraud; estimated fraud losses totaled $3.1 billion.
    2016 Small Business Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • The average identity theft victim dedicated 15 hours to resolving the crime
    2016 Fraud Report, Javelin Strategy & Research
  • Average of $13,450 in out-of-pocket costs and 200 hours spent to resolve medical identity theft
    2013 Study on Medical Identity Theft, Ponemon Institute
  • 120 days to resolve a typical tax identity theft case
    2015, Internal Revenue Service
  • 6% of identity theft complaints to the FTC involved children
    2014, Federal Trade Commission

Methods to Avoid

  • Outdated stats — more than 4 years old
  • Competitor reports
  • Unsourced statistics
  • Don’t use identity theft as an all-encompassing term, even if a report/study does. Identity theft is when a new account is opened in a victim’s name. Payment fraud is when existing accounts are abused. Lost/stolen PII is when information is unintentionally exposed. Use the term identity crimes to describe stats that address a wide array of threats to your good name.

Language that must be avoided

WHY GAP

  • ID Protection is not insurance so it should not be referred to as such.
  • Never use the word “coverage” or any other insurance-related terms.
  • You should not indicate that the $25,000 for a victim of identity theft is anything but a reimbursement.
  • Reference the EZShield Promise when referring to restoring a customer’s identity.
  • Do not use the following words:
    • Coverage
    • Insured
    • Claim
    • Liability
    • Policy
